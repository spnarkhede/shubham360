### 1. Value Ladder Architecture Designer

**Use Case**: Creating the structural backbone (Entry -> Core -> High-Ticket) for a sustainable business instead of just a "one-and-done" product.

**Target Personas**: Info Product Creator, Course Creator, Coach, Consultant

**Tags**: `value-ladder`, `business-model`, `strategy`, `lifetime-value`

**Prompt**:
```
I am an info product creator looking to move from a "one and done" product model to a sustainable value ladder.

My Current Product: [e.g., A $49 eBook on "How to Garden"]
My Target Audience: [e.g., Beginner urban gardeners]
My Core Transformation: [e.g., Helping people grow their own food without killing the plants]

Design a 4-rung Value Ladder Architecture for me:
1. **The Entry Rung (Lead Gen)**: A low-risk, low-cost offer ($7-$27) to attract cold traffic. What format should this be (quiz, mini-guide, checklist)?
2. **The Core Offer (The "Meat")**: My primary paid product ($97-$297) that delivers the main transformation. How should this be structured (course, membership, workshop)?
3. **The High-Ticket Rung (Max Profit)**: A deep transformation offer ($1,000-$5,000+) for my best students. What format fits here (coaching, mastermind, done-for-you)?
4. **The Ascension Path**: How do I logically move a customer from Rung 1 to Rung 4?

For each rung, define:
- The Specific Offer Name
- The Price Point
- The Delivery Format
- The "One Thing" it solves
```

### 2. Entry Rung (Lead Magnet) Generator

**Use Case**: Designing a "no-brainer" low-ticket offer ($7-$27) that acts as a trust-builder and lead generator, not a profit center.

**Target Personas**: Course Creator, Author, YouTuber, Newsletter Writer

**Tags**: `lead-magnet`, `low-ticket`, `tripwire`, `conversion`

**Prompt**:
```
I need to create an "Entry Rung" product for my info product business. This product is NOT for profit; it is to convert cold leads into buyers so I can upsell them later.

My Niche: [e.g., Keto Cooking]
My Main High-Ticket Offer: [e.g., 12-week Keto Coaching Program]

Generate 5 ideas for an Entry Rung product that:
1. Costs between $7 and $27.
2. Solves a specific, immediate "micro-problem" related to my niche.
3. Is easy to consume in under 60 minutes (PDF, Checklist, Mini-Video).
4. Creates a "cliffhanger" that makes them NEED my Core Offer.

For each idea, explain:
- What the product is.
- Why it works as a lead magnet.
- The specific "bridge" sentence to use in the sales page to pitch the Core Offer next.
```

### 3. Core Offer Transformation Architect

**Use Case**: Structuring the main course/program ($297-$997) so it delivers the actual result but leaves "room to grow" for the upsell.

**Target Personas**: Product Manager, Course Creator, Coach

**Tags**: `core-offer`, `curriculum`, `transformation`, `upsell`

**Prompt**:
```
I am defining my "Core Offer" (the main product on my value ladder).

The Promise: [e.g., "Learn to trade crypto profitably in 30 days"]
The Price: [e.g., $497]

Design the Core Offer structure using the "Open Loop" method:
1. **The "Quick Win" Module**: What can I teach in Module 1 that gives them a result immediately (so they trust me)?
2. **The Core Curriculum**: Outline the 3-5 main modules needed to deliver the full promise.
3. **The "Capability Gap"**: At the end of the course, what should they be ABLE to do, but what should they be MISSING (that only my High-Ticket offer provides)?

Output:
- A Module-by-Module Outline.
- The "Graduation Criteria" (what they have achieved).
- The "Bridge Pitch": A script explaining why they need the next level (High-Ticket) to get [Specific Missing Result].
```

### 4. High-Ticket "Irresistible Offer" Builder

**Use Case**: Creating a premium offer ($2,000+) that justifies the price through personalization, access, and speed—things a course can't provide.

**Target Personas**: High-Ticket Coach, Consultant, Agency Owner

**Tags**: `high-ticket`, `premium`, `coaching`, `irresistible-offer`

**Prompt**:
```
I need to create a High-Ticket Offer (Price: $2,000 - $10,000) to sit at the top of my value ladder.

My Core Offer is: [e.g., A self-paced course on "Real Estate Investing"]
My Target Student for this offer: [e.g., Doctors who have money but no time]

Design the High-Ticket Offer by filling in these blanks:
1. **The Mechanism**: Is this 1-on-1 coaching, a small group mastermind, or done-for-you?
2. **The "Done With You" vs "Done For You" split**: What do I do, and what do they do?
3. **The Guarantee**: What specific outcome can I guarantee because I am doing the work with them?
4. **The Scarcity**: Why can only 5-10 people join this per month?
5. **The Price Anchor**: How do I make $5,000 seem cheap compared to the $50,000 result they get?

Write the "Sales Page Headline" and "Sub-headline" for this offer.
```

### 5. Ladder Transition & Ascension Scriptwriter

**Use Case**: Writing the specific emails and scripts that move a customer from "Just bought the $27 ebook" -> "Buying the $497 course" without feeling "salesy."

**Target Personas**: Email Marketer, Copywriter, Funnel Builder

**Tags**: `email-marketing`, `nurture-sequence`, `ascension`, `copywriting`

**Prompt**:
```
I have a Value Ladder:
- Rung 1: $17 Checklist (Bought)
- Rung 2: $197 Course (Target)
- Rung 3: $2,000 Coaching (Target)

Write the "Ascension Email Sequence" for a customer who JUST bought the $17 Checklist.

Email 1 (Delivery + Quick Win): Deliver the checklist, give a quick tip, ask for a reply.
Email 2 (The Problem Agitation): "The checklist helps, but here is why you are still struggling with [Bigger Problem]..."
Email 3 (The Bridge): Introduce the $197 Course as the "system" that fixes the problem the checklist only hinted at.
Email 4 (The Logic): "Why $197 is cheaper than doing this yourself (Time vs Money math)."

Tone: Helpful expert, not sleazy salesman.
Goal: Get them to click "Buy" on the Core Offer.
```

### 6. Pricing Psychology & Strategy Map

**Use Case**: Setting price points for each rung so they feel like "no-brainers" relative to the value, using psychological anchors.

**Target Personas**: Founder, Business Strategist, Pricing Consultant

**Tags**: `pricing`, `psychology`, `anchoring`, `strategy`

**Prompt**:
```
I need to price my 3-rung Value Ladder using psychology.

My Market: [e.g., Freelance Graphic Designers]
My Goal: Maximize Lifetime Value (LTV).

1. **Entry Rung**: Should be "Impulse Buy" price. What is the sweet spot ($7, $17, $27, $47)? Explain the psychology.
2. **Core Rung**: Should be "Investment" price. How do I anchor this against the value of the result ($197, $497, $997)?
3. **High-Ticket Rung**: Should be "Transformation" price. How do I justify $3,000+? (ROI calculation).

Generate a Pricing Table:
| Rung | Product | Price | Psychological Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | ... | ... | ... |
| 2 | ... | ... | ... |
| 3 | ... | ... | ... |

Explain how the price jump from Rung 1 to 2 feels safe, and Rung 2 to 3 feels logical.
```

### 7. Product Pipeline Planner (MVP First)

**Use Case**: Deciding what to build *first* so you don't burn out trying to build the whole ladder at once.

**Target Personas**: Solopreneur, Creator, Startup Founder

**Tags**: `mvp`, `roadmap`, `pipeline`, `execution`

**Prompt**:
```
I want to build my Value Ladder, but I only have time to build ONE thing right now.

My Ladder Plan:
- Entry: $27 Template
- Core: $497 Course
- High: $3k Coaching

Act as a Product Strategist. Tell me exactly what to build first, second, and third.

For Step 1 (The MVP):
- What is the absolute simplest version of the Entry Rung I can launch in 7 days?
- How do I use this to validate if people even want the Core Offer?

For Step 2:
- When do I build the Core Offer? (Trigger: e.g., "After 50 Entry Rung sales")

For Step 3:
- When do I build the High Ticket? (Trigger: e.g., "After 10 Core Offer graduates ask for help")

Create a timeline/roadmap for me.
```

### 8. Ladder Performance Diagnostic

**Use Case**: Analyzing why your ladder is leaking money (e.g., people buy the cheap thing but never the expensive thing).

**Target Personas**: Business Consultant, Growth Marketer, Founder

**Tags**: `analytics`, `conversion-rate`, `diagnostic`, `optimization`

**Prompt**:
```
I am auditing my Value Ladder performance. Here are my numbers:

- Entry Rung Sales: 1,000
- Core Offer Sales: 10 (1% conversion)
- High-Ticket Sales: 1 (0.1% conversion)

Diagnose the "Leak" in my ladder.
1. **The Entry -> Core Leak**: Why are 990 people not buying the Core Offer? Is the price too high? Is the bridge weak? Is the Entry offer too good (they got what they needed)?
2. **The Core -> High Ticket Leak**: Why aren't my 10 Core students buying the $3k coaching?

Propose 3 specific experiments to fix these conversion rates.
- Experiment 1 (For Entry->Core): [e.g., Add a "Order Bump" in the checkout]
- Experiment 2 (For Core->High): [e.g., Change the upsell timing to Week 3 of the course]
```
