- **Budgeting**

- **Why it’s important for gig workers**: Helps manage irregular income and plan for periods of low work.

- **Step-by-Step Guidance** 
- **Why it’s important**: Provides clear, actionable steps that learners can follow. 
- **Example**: Break down complex concepts into simple, actionable steps. For example, guide users through creating a budget in a step-by-step format.

- **Why they are important**: Helps reinforce learning and encourages consistent financial management. 
- **Example**: Send regular reminders about financial health checks, such as reviewing budgets or checking savings progress.

- **Irregular Income** 
- **Explanation**: Gig workers often have fluctuating income, which requires flexible financial planning. 
- **Solution**: Teach techniques for managing irregular income, such as creating a buffer fund and using conservative budgeting. 
- **Lack of Traditional Benefits** 
- **Explanation**: Gig workers often miss out on benefits like health insurance and retirement plans. 
- **Solution**: Provide guidance on self-insurance options, such as health savings accounts (HSAs) and individual retirement accounts (IRAs). 
- **Financial Planning for the Future** 
- **Explanation**: Gig workers need to plan for long-term financial goals without the support of employer-sponsored programs. 
- **Solution**: Offer resources on investing, retirement planning, and building wealth over time.
