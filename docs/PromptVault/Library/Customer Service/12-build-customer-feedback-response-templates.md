```

* **Purpose**: Gather essential details about your industry, product/service, and common feedback types.

  2. List the most common types of customer feedback your team receives.

* **Output**: Summary of your business context to guide template creation.
* **Transition**: Use this context to inform phase 2.

* **Purpose**: Establish a flexible template framework to be used across scenarios.
* **Research Needs**: Gather industry best practices for customer response.

* **Processing**: Define sections for each template (Template Name, When to Use, The Template, Personalization Tips, What to Avoid).
* **Output**: A structured format for all templates, ensuring clarity and usability.
* **Transition**: Prepare to develop content for each template scenario.

##PHASE 3: Creating Specific Templates

* **Purpose**: Develop response templates for different feedback scenarios.
* **Research Needs**: None; build on the gathered context.
```

### What it does

- Guides through building a customer feedback response template library across 8 common service scenarios.
- Gathers business context including industry details, product type, and typical feedback patterns the team encounters.
- Delivers ready-to-use response templates with personalization guidance, usage triggers, and mistake-avoidance tips for each scenario.

### Tips

- Audit your last 50 customer feedback interactions before using this AI prompt to identify which tone patterns and resolution approaches actually worked with your audience.
- Answer the business context section with specific customer language and real complaint examples rather than general descriptions to generate templates that match your actual support scenarios.
- After receiving your template library, have three experienced agents test each template in live conversations for one week and document which personalization tips need adjustment based on customer responses.

### How to use

1. Run the full prompt providing detailed context about your business in the bracketed section, including your industry, product or service type, and the most common customer feedback patterns your team encounters.
2. Example: "Industry: SaaS project management software, Product: Team collaboration platform with 50K users, Common feedback types: Feature requests for integrations, complaints about mobile app bugs, billing questions about team seat pricing, praise for onboarding experience"
