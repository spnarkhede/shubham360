> Create effective onboarding email sequences with this AI prompt, including welcome messages, quick wins, value delivery, social proof, and personalized check-ins.

### Prompt

```
# CONTEXT:
Adopt the role of lifecycle email strategist. A new user just signed up for the product but hasn't experienced its core value yet. Most onboarding sequences fail because they overwhelm users with features instead of guiding them to their first meaningful outcome. The user is in a critical 14-day window where they'll either become an active user or abandon the product entirely. Previous generic welcome emails already get ignored. You have five precisely-timed touchpoints to transform a skeptical signup into someone who genuinely understands why this product matters to their specific situation.

# ROLE:
You're a former SaaS product manager who sent 10,000 users through broken onboarding funnels, watched 73% churn before day 30, and obsessively A/B tested every word until you discovered that onboarding isn't about explaining featuresâ€”it's about engineering tiny victories that compound into habit formation. You now write email sequences that feel like a patient mentor guiding someone through their first week at a new job, not a marketing department shouting about capabilities.

# RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
Structure each of the five emails as a complete, ready-to-send message. Begin with Email 1 and progress chronologically through Email 5, clearly labeling each email's timing and purpose. For every email, provide three distinct components: the subject line (designed to get opened), the preview text (under 90 characters, reinforcing the open), and the full email body (the actual message content). Each email should build on the previous one, creating a narrative arc from "I just signed up" to "I understand this product's value in my life." The sequence should feel like a conversation unfolding over two weeks, not five disconnected broadcasts. Each email must accomplish its specific psychological job: Email 1 reduces anxiety and provides one clear first step, Email 2 delivers an early accomplishment, Email 3 connects features to real outcomes, Email 4 validates their decision through others' success, and Email 5 opens a human connection point.

# TASK CRITERIA:
1. Each email must stay under 180 wordsâ€”no exceptions. Brevity forces clarity.
2. Tone must remain warm and conversational throughout, like a knowledgeable friend, never corporate or salesy.
3. Each email contains exactly one clear call-to-action. Multiple CTAs create decision paralysis.
4. Avoid hard selling in any email. The goal is activation, not persuasion.
5. Subject lines should create curiosity or promise value, not sound like marketing.
6. Preview text must complement the subject line, not repeat it.
7. Email bodies should use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max) and natural language.
8. Each email must serve its designated purpose: Welcome, Quick Win, Core Value, Social Proof, or Check-In.
9. The sequence should acknowledge the user's specific context and goals, not generic benefits.
10. Avoid feature dumps, jargon, or assuming the user remembers why they signed up.
11. Each email should feel personal and timely, not automated and generic.
12. The final email must genuinely invite response, not fake personalization.

# INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
- My product/service: [INSERT YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE]
- My target customer: [INSERT TARGET CUSTOMER DESCRIPTION]
- My product's core value: [INSERT THE MAIN BENEFIT/OUTCOME YOUR PRODUCT DELIVERS]

# RESPONSE FORMAT:
Present each email as a complete unit with clear headers indicating timing and purpose. Format each email with:

**Email [Number] (Timing): [Purpose]**

**Subject Line:** [The subject line]

**Preview Text:** [Under 90 characters]

**Email Body:**
[The complete email text with natural paragraph breaks]

[Clear CTA]

Separate each email with a line break for visual clarity. Write all five emails in sequence, maintaining consistent voice and building narrative momentum from first contact through day 14 check-in.
```

### What it does

- Analyzes an AI prompt that creates a 5-email onboarding sequence for new customers.
- Explains how the AI prompt guides users to write welcome emails that turn signups into active users.
- Shows what structure the AI prompt follows to deliver emails with subject lines, preview text, and body content.

### Tips

- Test your AI prompt with different product types and customer personas to see how the sequence adapts, then refine the context section with specific details about your actual audience\
