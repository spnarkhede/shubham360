# 📋 Customer Success Plan AI Prompts for CSMs

> **TL;DR**: AI helps CSMs scale high-touch relationship management. Automation handles routine tasks; humans handle relationships. Account planning benefits from AI synthesis. Risk identification improves with pattern analysis. Expansion opportunities scale with AI pattern matching. AI doesn't replace CSM judgment — it augments it. Effective prompts require account context. The more context you provide, the better the output.

## 📝 Prompt 1. Account Plan Generation

**Use Case**: You need a full account plan for a customer — but you don't want to start from scratch. Feed AI all the account context and get a draft with executive summary, strategic priorities, engagement plan, success metrics, risk watchlist, and expansion opportunities — all ready for your human refinement.

**Target Personas**: Customer Success Managers, CSM Team Leads, VP of Customer Success

**Tags**: `account-planning`, `strategy-draft`, `CSM-workflow`, `AI-augmented`

**Prompt**:
```
I need to generate an account plan for [customer name].

Account context:
- Company: [description]
- Industry: [sector]
- Size: [employee count, revenue if known]
- Key contacts: [names and roles]

Relationship history:
- Customer since: [tenure]
- Key milestones: [accomplishments together]
- Previous issues: [any problems they've had]
- Current status: [health/risk assessment]

Product usage:
- What they use: [products/modules]
- Usage patterns: [intensity, breadth]
- Key features: [what they rely on most]

Generate an account plan that includes:
1. Executive summary (account health, risks, opportunities)
2. Strategic priorities for next quarter
3. Engagement plan (touchpoints, Cadence)
4. Success metrics (what a successful quarter looks like)
5. Risk watchlist (what to monitor)
6. Expansion opportunities (where growth might exist)
7. Notes on key stakeholder dynamics

This draft needs your expertise to finalize—fill gaps and correct where AI got it wrong.
```

## 💬 FAQ

| Question | Short Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| **Does AI replace CSM judgment?** | No. AI augments CSM work — drafting, synthesis, pattern identification. Strategic decisions, relationship judgment, complex negotiations remain human. AI is a tool, not a replacement. |
| **How do I ensure AI-generated content sounds human?** | Provide rich context. The more specific you are about the customer, their situation, and your relationship, the more personalized the output. Always review and refine. Add your voice, your memory, your judgment. AI drafts; humans finalize. |
| **What CSM tasks shouldn't use AI?** | Highly sensitive situations (executive coaching on culture, serious performance concerns), situations requiring human dynamics judgment (escalation timing, political situations), and anything requiring confidentiality beyond what's appropriate to share with AI. |
| **How do I manage data privacy with AI tools?** | Use AI tools that comply with your data policies. Don't share sensitive account info (competitive vulnerabilities, security concerns) unless you're certain about their security practices. When in doubt, keep it out. |
| **What's the biggest mistake CSMs make with AI?** | Using AI output without human review. AI drafts quickly, but drafts aren't final. Always review for accuracy, appropriateness, and alignment with your relationship and strategy. AI generates; CSMs approve. |
| **How do I measure AI impact on CSM productivity?** | Track time spent on routine tasks vs. strategic relationship activities. If AI is working, you should see time shift toward relationship management and strategic work. Monitor account health — AI should help you serve accounts better, not just faster. |
| **What's the future of AI in CS?** | AI handles more synthesis and drafting. Human judgment becomes more valuable as AI handles routine work. CSMs who learn to work with AI will outperform those who resist it. The role evolves toward strategic relationship management. |

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls to Avoid

| Pitfall | Why It Hurts | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| **Using AI output without review** | AI makes confident-sounding mistakes. Wrong names, wrong data, wrong tone. | Always review for accuracy, appropriateness, and alignment. AI generates; CSMs approve. |
| **Not providing enough context** | Vague input = vague output. "Help me with my account" produces garbage. | Feed rich context: company, history, usage, relationship, goals. The more you give, the better you get. |
| **Trying to automate everything** | Some tasks need human judgment — escalation timing, political navigation, emotional conversations. | Use AI for drafting, synthesis, pattern ID. Keep strategic decisions human. |
| **Ignoring data privacy** | Sharing competitive vulnerabilities or security concerns with AI creates risk. | Don't share sensitive info unless you're certain about the AI tool's security practices. When in doubt, keep it out. |
| **Measuring time saved instead of outcomes improved** | Saving 2 hours means nothing if account health got worse. | Track account health metrics, expansion rates, churn reduction. AI should help you serve accounts better, not just faster. |
| **Using AI during live customer calls without prep** | Real-time AI feels robotic. Customers notice. | Use AI for PREP only — drafts, talking points, context. During calls, be human. |
