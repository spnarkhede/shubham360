## The Roadmap Conversation Mindset (Quick Read)

| ❌ What It's NOT | ✅ What It IS |
|---|---|
| Status update ("Here's what shipped") | Strategic alignment ("Does our direction match your needs?") |
| Feature marketing ("Here's everything we're building") | Partnership validation ("Are we investing where you need us to?") |
| Q&A session ("Any questions?") | Opportunity identification ("What would make this more valuable?") |

> 💡 You're not presenting TO customers — you're having a dialogue WITH them.

## 1. Pre-Meeting Research Prompts

> Good roadmap conversations start with understanding the customer — not the roadmap.

---

### 1. AI Prompt for Customer Context Preparation

**Use Case**: You have a roadmap review coming up and need a strategic briefing — not just a feature list.

**Target Personas**: CSMs, Account Managers, Customer Success Directors

**Tags**: `pre-meeting-prep`, `customer-context`, `roadmap-briefing`, `AI-research`

**Prompt**:
```
I have a roadmap review coming up with [customer name].

Their company context:
[paste or describe what you know about their business]

Their role:
[paste or describe their role and priorities]

Our relationship history:
[paste or describe previous touchpoints, feedback, issues]

What I know about their goals:
[paste or describe what they're trying to achieve with our product]

What they care about most:
[paste or describe what matters to them personally]

Generate a pre-meeting briefing that:
1. Summarizes their context for the conversation
2. Identifies which roadmap items likely matter most to them
3. Surfaces questions they might ask based on their situation
4. Notes what they've mentioned in the past that should influence my framing
5. Flags areas where I need to manage expectations

Good preparation transforms generic presentations into strategic conversations.
```

## 7. FAQ

| Question | Short Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| **How often should roadmap reviews happen?** | Strategic accounts = quarterly. High-velocity = bi-monthly. Lower-engagement = semi-annually. Let relationship depth guide frequency. |
| **Full roadmap or curated items?** | Curate. Show them what affects THEIR goals. Full roadmaps overwhelm and dilute the message. |
| **What if we don't have exciting roadmap items?** | Be honest. "This quarter is about stability and performance." Share next quarter to maintain anticipation. Transparency builds trust. |
| **How do I handle requests I can't fulfill?** | Be honest. Explain why. Offer alternatives. Escalate if needed. Customers respect honesty over evasiveness. |
| **Should customers see the full roadmap timeline?** | 6-month visibility for strategic accounts. Beyond that, it changes too much to be reliable. |
| **How do I make it feel like dialogue, not presentation?** | Ask questions first. Present second. Ask what they thought after. Make space for their reactions. |
| **What if a customer is clearly unhappy with the roadmap?** | Dig deeper. Unhappiness = misalignment, competitive pressure, or unmet needs. It's a conversation starter, not a verdict. |
