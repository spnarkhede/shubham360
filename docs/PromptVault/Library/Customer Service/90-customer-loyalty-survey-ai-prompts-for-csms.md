## 1. Understanding Loyalty Dimensions (Quick Read)

| Dimension | What It Measures | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Product Loyalty** | Does the product do what they need? Is it reliable? | Drives willingness to keep using the product |
| **Relationship Loyalty** | Do they trust their CSM? Feel valued? | Matters even when product has issues — gives you benefit of the doubt |
| **Support Loyalty** | When they need help, do they get it? Fast? Effective? | Often matters MORE than product loyalty during a crisis |
| **Value Loyalty** | Is the value worth the price? ROI belief? | Drives renewal and expansion. Poor value = churn predictor |
| **Strategic Loyalty** | Does your roadmap align with their long-term goals? | Matters for long-term partnerships and expansion |

> 💡 High product loyalty + low relationship loyalty = retention risk when a competitor offers better service. High relationship loyalty + low value loyalty = churn when budget pressure hits. Measure ALL five.

## 2. Persona-Based Survey Design Prompts

> Different personas need different questions. A VP of Sales cares about pipeline impact. An IT admin cares about uptime. Same customer, different levers.

---

### 1. AI Prompt for Persona Loyalty Survey

**Use Case**: You need a loyalty survey tailored to a specific persona — not a generic "how likely are you to recommend us" template.

**Target Personas**: CSMs, Customer Success Directors, VoC Program Managers, Product Managers

**Tags**: `persona-survey`, `loyalty-by-role`, `targeted-questions`, `AI-survey`

**Prompt**:
```
I want to design a loyalty survey for [persona type—e.g., sponsor sponsor, IT admin, end-user].

Persona characteristics:
- Role: [their job responsibilities]
- What they care about: [their priorities]
- Their usage of our product: [how they interact]
- Their relationship with us: [how long, what touchpoints]

Loyalty drivers for this persona:
- [what typically drives their loyalty in a vendor relationship]
- [what typically causes them to leave a vendor]

What I want to learn from the survey:
[what decisions this survey should inform]

Generate a loyalty survey for this persona that:
1. Uses language and framing appropriate to their role
2. Focuses on dimensions that matter to THIS persona
3. Includes specific questions that reveal actionable insights
4. Is appropriately scoped (not too long for their role)
5. Uses scales that reveal gradations, not just pass/fail

Executive sponsors don't want to answer 30 questions about UI usability.
End users don't care about contract terms.
```

## 3. Multi-Dimensional Loyalty Prompts

> Loyalty is not a single construct. Break it into dimensions to reveal where you're strong and where you're at risk.

---

### 3. AI Prompt for Comprehensive Loyalty Survey

**Use Case**: You want a survey that measures ALL loyalty dimensions — not just product satisfaction.

**Target Personas**: CSMs, Customer Success Directors, VoC Program Managers, BI Teams

**Tags**: `comprehensive-loyalty`, `multi-dimensional`, `5-dimensions`, `AI-survey`

**Prompt**:
```
I want to create a comprehensive loyalty survey covering multiple dimensions.

Customer: [company and relationship context]
Stakeholder: [who would take this survey]

Dimensions to cover:
1. Product satisfaction
2. Relationship quality
3. Support experience
4. Value perception
5. Strategic alignment

Generate a loyalty survey that:
1. Includes 2-3 questions per dimension (not just one)
2. Uses consistent scales where possible for cross-dimension comparison
3. Includes a Net Promoter Score question for benchmark comparison
4. Has an open-ended question for unfiltered feedback
5. Is short enough to complete in 5 minutes
6. Flows logically from one dimension to the next

Multi-dimensional loyalty surveys reveal where you're strong and where you're at risk.
```

## 8. FAQ

| Question | Short Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| **How often should I measure loyalty?** | At minimum, annually for all customers. For high-value or at-risk accounts: quarterly or continuous pulse surveys. Consistent measurement = trend tracking. |
| **What's a good loyalty score?** | Context > absolute numbers. A 7/10 means different things by industry, history, and competition. Focus on TRENDS: improving or declining? Are your most valuable customers also your most loyal? |
| **Should I share loyalty scores with the customer?** | Sometimes. Sharing drives accountability. But sharing LOW scores without a clear improvement plan can accelerate churn. If you share, pair the score with a plan. |
| **How do I handle customers who won't complete surveys?** | For non-respondents, use behavioral signals (usage, engagement) as loyalty proxies. For strategic accounts that won't survey, have direct conversations. Survey refusers might be signaling disengagement. |
| **What if loyalty scores contradict other signals?** | Loyalty surveys are ONE input. If usage is strong but survey scores are low — dig into why. Maybe the respondent doesn't represent the full account. Use multiple signals together. |
| **How do I avoid survey fatigue?** | Keep surveys short. Only survey when you have genuine questions. Personalize the ask (from their CSM, not "the team"). Show you acted on previous feedback. |
| **Can AI replace human judgment in loyalty assessment?** | AI helps analyze patterns and flag risks. But human judgment is essential for interpretation: context, relationships, escalation timing. Use AI for efficiency; humans for decisions. |
