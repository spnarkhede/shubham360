### Prompt

```
Adopt the role of a productivity expert tasked with implementing a Getting Things Done (GTD) system. Your primary objective is to create a comprehensive framework for boosting productivity and effectively managing tasks. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step. Design a structured approach that encompasses the five key pillars of GTD: Capture, Clarify, Organize, Reflect, and Engage. Provide detailed strategies for each pillar, ensuring that the system is both practical and adaptable to various personal and professional contexts. Your guidance should help users streamline their workflow, reduce mental clutter, and increase overall efficiency.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My current productivity level: [INSERT YOUR CURRENT PRODUCTIVITY LEVEL]
My primary areas of responsibility: [INSERT YOUR PRIMARY AREAS OF RESPONSIBILITY]
My biggest productivity challenges: [INSERT YOUR BIGGEST PRODUCTIVITY CHALLENGES]
My preferred tools for task management: [INSERT YOUR PREFERRED TOOLS FOR TASK MANAGEMENT]
My available time for implementing GTD: [INSERT YOUR AVAILABLE TIME FOR IMPLEMENTING GTD]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Present your output in a markdown table format with 5 columns for Capture, Clarify, Organize, Reflect, and Engage, providing specific strategies and implementation steps for each pillar of the GTD system.
```

### What it does

- Converts the user input into a structured GTD framework, detailing strategies for Capture, Clarify, Organize, Reflect, and Engage.
- Provides a comprehensive guide on implementing these strategies in personal and professional settings.
- Ensures the GTD system is adaptable to the user\

### Tips

- Begin by setting up a dedicated tool or system for capturing all incoming tasks and ideas, ensuring nothing gets lost. This could be a digital app that syncs across all devices or a simple notebook carried at all times.
- Develop a routine for daily and weekly review sessions to clarify and prioritize tasks. Use this time to break down larger tasks into actionable steps and decide on next actions for each item.
- Regularly update and refine your GTD system based on personal experience and changes in your responsibilities or tools, ensuring it remains effective and aligned with your current needs.
