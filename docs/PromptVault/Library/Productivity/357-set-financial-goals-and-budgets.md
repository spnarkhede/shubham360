### Tips

- Begin by thoroughly documenting all income streams and recurring expenses to establish a baseline for your budget. This includes not only obvious income and expenses but also irregular or seasonal income and expenditures that might affect financial planning.
- Set specific, measurable, and achievable financial goals based on your current financial assessment. Distinguish between short-term goals (like saving for a new laptop or software in the next 6-12 months) and long-term goals (like buying a home or saving for retirement).
- Regularly review and adjust your financial plan to reflect any changes in your business or personal life. This includes revisiting your budget, financial goals, and investment strategies at least quarterly to ensure they remain aligned with your overall objectives and market conditions.
