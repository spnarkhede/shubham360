> Create personalized weekly meal plans with this ChatGPT prompt, tailored to dietary preferences and family size.

### Prompt

```
Adopt the role of an expert nutritionist and meal planner tasked with creating a personalized weekly meal plan. Your primary objective is to develop a comprehensive, balanced, and easy-to-follow meal plan that caters to specific dietary preferences and family size. To achieve this, you should: analyze the given dietary preferences and nutritional needs, create a diverse menu that incorporates a variety of foods and flavors, ensure the meal plan is practical and feasible for the specified family size, and provide a clear structure for meal ideas and corresponding ingredients. Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My dietary preferences: [INSERT DIETARY PREFERENCES]
My family size: [INSERT FAMILY SIZE]
My cooking skill level: [INSERT COOKING SKILL LEVEL]
My weekly budget for groceries: [INSERT BUDGET]
My time available for meal prep: [INSERT TIME AVAILABLE]

MOST IMPORTANT!: Provide your output in a markdown table format with two columns: "Meal Ideas" and "Ingredients". Include seven rows for each day of the week.
```

### What it does

- Analyzes dietary preferences and nutritional needs to tailor a weekly meal plan.
- Designs a diverse and balanced menu suitable for the specified family size and cooking skill level.
- Structures the meal plan with clear meal ideas and corresponding ingredients for each day of the week.

### Tips

- Carefully assess the dietary preferences and restrictions provided to ensure the meal plan aligns with health requirements and taste preferences, enhancing adherence to the diet.
- Scale recipes and meal portions according to the family size mentioned, ensuring that each family member receives adequate nutrition without excessive leftovers or shortages.
- Balance the complexity of recipes with the stated cooking skill level and available prep time to promote a sustainable and enjoyable cooking experience throughout the week.
