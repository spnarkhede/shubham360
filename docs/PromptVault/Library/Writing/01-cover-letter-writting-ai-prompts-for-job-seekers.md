### 1. Understanding AI Assistance for Cover Letters

**Use Case**: When you want to know exactly what AI can and cannot do for your cover letter before starting

**Target Personas**: Job seekers, career changers, freelancers

**Tags**: `AI-basics`, `cover-letter`, `beginner-friendly`

**Prompt**:

```
Explain how AI can assist cover letter writing effectively:

Strengths of AI for cover letters:

1. **Structure and format**:
   - Professional letter structure
   - Paragraph organization
   - Transition suggestions

2. **Language refinement**:
   - Vocabulary enhancement
   - Sentence variety
   - Tone calibration

3. **Content generation**:
   - First draft generation from bullet points
   - Multiple variations
   - Quantification suggestions

4. **Research synthesis**:
   - Company research integration
   - Job description keyword incorporation
   - Industry language

Where AI struggles:

1. **Authentic voice**: Your unique experiences and perspective
2. **Personal anecdotes**: Specific stories that demonstrate qualities
3. **Genuine enthusiasm**: Real emotional connection to the opportunity
4. **Current information**: Recent company news or developments

Best practices for AI use:
- Use AI as a starting point, not the final product
- Always personalize AI output
- Maintain your authentic voice
- Verify all factual claims

Generate guidance on effective AI use in cover letter writing.
```

### 2. Human-AI Collaboration Workflow

**Use Case**: When you want a step-by-step workflow that combines AI speed with human authenticity

**Target Personas**: Job seekers, recruiters, career coaches

**Tags**: `workflow`, `human-AI-collab`, `process`

**Prompt**:

```
Develop a human-AI cover letter workflow:

Phase 1: Preparation (Human)

Before using AI:

1. **Research the company**:
   - Company mission and values
   - Recent news and developments
   - Products or services
   - Culture and environment

2. **Analyze the job**:
   - Required qualifications
   - Preferred qualifications
   - Key responsibilities
   - Company-specific language

3. **Reflect on yourself**:
   - Relevant experiences
   - Specific achievements
   - Transferable skills
   - Genuine interest indicators

Phase 2: Drafting (AI-assisted)

AI helps with:
- Generating initial structure
- Suggesting language
- Creating variations
- Refining wording

Phase 3: Personalization (Human)

Add your authentic touch:
- Specific examples and stories
- Genuine enthusiasm
- Personal connection to company
- Your unique perspective

Phase 4: Refinement (Human with AI assistance)

Final polish:
- Read aloud for flow
- Check authenticity
- Verify facts
- Ensure match to job

Generate a complete workflow template.
```

### 3. Basic Cover Letter Draft Generator

**Use Case**: When you need a solid first draft fast based on your background and job requirements

**Target Personas**: Job seekers, freshers, career switchers

**Tags**: `draft-generator`, `basic-draft`, `ATS-friendly`

**Prompt**:

```
Generate a cover letter draft for the following position:

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Position: [JOB TITLE]
Job description key requirements: [KEY REQUIREMENTS FROM JD]

My background:
- Current/title previous role: [ROLE AND COMPANY]
- Years of experience: [NUMBER]
- Key relevant skills: [LIST SKILLS]
- Key relevant achievements: [LIST ACHIEVEMENTS WITH NUMBERS]
- Education: [DEGREE AND FIELD]

Write a professional cover letter that:
1. Opens with a compelling hook about the role/company
2. Demonstrates knowledge of the company and role
3. Connects my background to their needs
4. Includes specific quantifiable achievements
5. Closes with clear next steps

Use professional but conversational tone.
Length should be approximately 300-400 words.
```

### 20. Common Mistakes Checker Prompt

**Use Case**: When you want to catch and fix the most common cover letter blunders before submitting

**Target Personas**: Job seekers, self-reviewers, first-time applicants

**Tags**: `mistake-checker`, `quality-control`, `common-errors`

**Prompt**:

```
Review this cover letter for common mistakes:

Draft: [PASTE DRAFT]

Common mistakes to check:

1. **Generic openings**: "I am excited to apply..."
2. **Resume restatement**: Just listing job duties
3. **Lack of specificity**: Vague claims without examples
4. **Wrong company references**: Generic company flattery
5. **Negative framing**: Explaining why you left or lack experience
6. **Tone issues**: Too formal, too casual, or inconsistent
7. **Length problems**: Too long or too short
8. **Missing elements**: No clear fit demonstration

For each mistake found:
- Identify the specific problem
- Suggest how to fix it
- Explain why this matters

Provide revised version fixing identified issues.
```
