### 1. Basic Cover Letter Prompt

**Use Case**: When you need a solid, structured cover letter fast by feeding ChatGPT your job details and background

**Target Personas**: Job seekers, freshers, career switchers, active applicants

**Tags**: `basic-draft`, `foundation`, `quick-start`, `ChatGPT`

**Prompt**:

```
Write a cover letter for [job title] at [company name]. The job description emphasizes: [key requirements from job posting]. My relevant background: [your experience and accomplishments]. I am particularly interested in this role because: [why this company/role interests you]. Make the letter compelling, specific, and under 400 words. Follow the structure: opening hook, value proposition, company fit, confident close.
```

### 2. Accomplishment-Focused Prompt

**Use Case**: When your strongest assets are specific wins with numbers — and you want the letter built around them

**Target Personas**: Experienced professionals, quantifiers, results-driven applicants

**Tags**: `accomplishments`, `metrics-driven`, `results-first`, `ChatGPT`

**Prompt**:

```
Write a cover letter that emphasizes my specific accomplishments: [describe 2-3 key accomplishments with metrics where possible]. These accomplishments are relevant to [job requirements]. Connect each accomplishment to the value I will create at [company]. The tone should be confident without being arrogant.
```

### 3. Entry-Level Cover Letter Prompt

**Use Case**: When you have little to no work experience but still need a killer cover letter that doesn't highlight gaps

**Target Personas**: Freshers, interns, graduates, junior applicants

**Tags**: `entry-level`, `no-experience`, `freshers`, `ChatGPT`

**Prompt**:

```
Write a cover letter for [entry-level job title] at [company]. I have limited professional experience but: [relevant education, projects, internships, transferable skills]. The job requires: [key requirements]. Frame my background positively by emphasizing eagerness to learn, adaptability, and relevant academic or project experience.
```

### 4. Technical Role Cover Letter Prompt

**Use Case**: When you're applying for a dev/engineer/data role and need to balance technical depth with business value

**Target Personas**: Developers, engineers, data scientists, IT professionals, architects

**Tags**: `technical`, `developer`, `engineering`, `tech-roles`, `ChatGPT`

**Prompt**:

```
Write a cover letter for a [specific technical role — e.g., software engineer, data scientist] at [company]. My technical skills include: [specific technologies, languages, methodologies]. My most relevant projects: [describe with technical details]. How my technical background creates value for this company: [specific connection]. Emphasize problem-solving ability and technical depth.
```

**Tags**: `career-change`, `pivot`, `transferable-skills`, `ChatGPT`

### 13. Employment Gap Cover Letter Prompt

**Use Case**: When you have a gap in your resume and need to address it head-on without being defensive

**Target Personas**: Gap-year returners, parents returning, health recovery, career break takers

**Tags**: `employment-gap`, `career-break`, `honest-framing`, `ChatGPT`

**Prompt**:

```
Write a cover letter that addresses an employment gap of [duration] due to [reason — family, health, education, etc.]. Focus on: what I did during the gap that maintained or developed relevant skills, how this experience actually prepared me for [role], and my enthusiasm for returning to [field/role].
```

### 16. Voice Personalization Prompt

**Use Case**: When your AI draft sounds robotic and you need to inject your real voice back in

**Target Personas**: All job seekers, editors, self-reviewers, authenticity seekers

**Tags**: `voice-match`, `authenticity`, `human-touch`, `ChatGPT`

**Prompt**:

```
Here is a cover letter draft: [paste draft]. Here is how I naturally speak: [provide 2-3 sentences of your own writing]. Rewrite the letter in my voice using these examples as a style guide. Maintain the structure and content but make it sound authentically like me.
```

### 18. Length Reduction Prompt

**Use Case**: When your cover letter is too long and you need to trim it without losing the key parts

**Target Personas**: Word-counters, concise communicators, format-followers

**Tags**: `trim`, `length-check`, `concise`, `word-count`, `ChatGPT`

**Prompt**:

```
Here is a cover letter that is [X words]. Reduce it to [target words] while maintaining: the opening hook, at least one specific accomplishment, the company fit paragraph, and the confident close. Cut filler without losing substance.
```

### 19. Tone Calibration Prompt

**Use When your draft feels off — too stiff, too casual, or too arrogant — and you need to dial it in

**Target Personas**: Tone-sensitive applicants, brand-aware job seekers, professional communicators

**Tags**: `tone-check`, `calibration`, `professional-balance`, `ChatGPT`

**Prompt**:

```
Review this cover letter for tone: [paste letter]. Is the tone confident without being arrogant? Specific without being dry? Professional without being stiff? Suggest specific improvements to make it more engaging while maintaining professionalism.
```

### 20. Opening Hook Generator Prompt

**Use Case**: When your opening line is boring and you need 5 fresh alternatives to pick from

**Target Personas**: All job seekers, hook-hunters, attention-grabbers

**Tags**: `opening-hook`, `first-line`, `attention-grabber`, `ChatGPT`

**Prompt**:

```
Generate 5 different opening hooks for a cover letter about [role] at [company]. Each hook should: be different from the others (different angles, different tones), grab attention in the first line, lead naturally into the value proposition. I will choose the one that best fits my voice.
```

### 21. Keyword Optimization Prompt

**Use Case**: When you want to make sure your cover letter passes ATS filters and hits all the right keywords

**Target Personas**: ATS-conscious applicants, corporate job seekers, large-company applicants

**Tags**: `ATS`, `keyword-optimization`, `screening-ready`, `ChatGPT`

**Prompt**:

```
Review this cover letter against the job description: [paste letter and job description]. Identify: keywords from the job description that should appear in the letter, skills or requirements mentioned in the job posting that are not addressed, and specific phrases that should be incorporated to pass ATS screening.
```

### 22. Final Polish Prompt

**Use Case**: When your draft is done and you need a last-pass quality check before hitting submit

**Target Personas**: Final-stage applicants, perfectionists, submission-ready job seekers

**Tags**: `final-polish`, `last-check`, `submission-ready`, `ChatGPT`

**Prompt**:

```
Perform a final review of this cover letter: [paste letter]. Check for: grammar and spelling errors, repetition of resume content, generic statements that could apply to any job, passive voice overuse, and anything that sounds inauthentic. Provide specific corrections.
```
